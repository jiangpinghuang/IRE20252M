# Code of Conduct

This project supports research, teaching, and open collaboration. Participants are expected to communicate respectfully, review work constructively, and keep discussions focused on evidence, reproducibility, and research quality.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat collaborators, students, reviewers, and external contributors with respect.
- Give feedback that is specific, actionable, and grounded in project goals.
- Credit ideas, datasets, code, and writing contributions appropriately.
- Surface risks, limitations, and validity threats clearly.
- Respect privacy, data usage limits, and institutional policies.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, intimidation, or discriminatory language.
- Misrepresentation of authorship, data provenance, results, or experimental conditions.
- Sharing private, sensitive, or restricted data without approval.
- Disruptive conduct that prevents others from contributing productively.

## Reporting

Concerns should be raised with the project advisor or repository maintainers. Reports should be handled with care, confidentiality where appropriate, and a focus on restoring a productive research environment.
