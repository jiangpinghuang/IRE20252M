\begin{abstract}
Natural language requirements often omit essential information and contain ambiguity during elicitation.
This paper studies multi-agent requirement clarification via missing information detection.
The proposed research framework coordinates interviewer, end-user, and analyst agents to detect missing functional, constraint, role, data, and exception information, then uses knowledge-guided clarification templates to ask targeted follow-up questions.
The study is scoped to G1-elicitation and evaluates whether this workflow improves requirement completeness, missing information coverage, clarification effectiveness, and human effort.
\end{abstract}
