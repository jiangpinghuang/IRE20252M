\begin{abstract}
Natural language requirements often contain unrealistic goals, infeasible constraints, and implicit assumptions about resources, performance, and deployment environments.
This paper studies requirement feasibility as a first-class requirements engineering problem.
We propose a research framework for knowledge-guided feasibility analysis in which multiple specialized agents inspect candidate requirements, reason over constraint categories, and surface unrealistic or underspecified assumptions for human validation.
The paper is structured around requirement realism verification rather than system construction: it defines the target problem, outlines a multi-agent verification workflow, and specifies an evaluation plan for measuring feasibility detection, constraint verification, and human review reduction.
\end{abstract}
