\begin{abstract}
Natural language requirements are readable for stakeholders but are often difficult to execute directly because they lack explicit action semantics, behavioral mappings, and task-level dependencies.
This paper studies the problem of transforming natural language requirements into executable task workflows for intelligent software systems.
We outline a knowledge-guided multi-agent planning approach that analyzes requirement intent, constraints, actions, and execution dependencies, then constructs an actionable requirement representation through task decomposition, sequence generation, and human review.
The proposed study is evaluated around task generation accuracy, execution completeness, requirement-to-task alignment, and planning consistency.
\end{abstract}
