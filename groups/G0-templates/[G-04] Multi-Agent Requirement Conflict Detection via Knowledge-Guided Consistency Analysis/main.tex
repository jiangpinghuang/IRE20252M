\begin{abstract}
Natural language requirements often contain semantic conflicts, logical contradictions,
inconsistent constraints, role-level disagreements, and implicit incompatibilities.
This paper studies requirement conflict detection as a focused software engineering
problem and proposes a knowledge-guided multi-agent workflow for consistency
verification. The intended approach combines collaborative requirement analysis,
constraint-aware conflict identification, explanation-oriented validation, and
human-in-the-loop refinement. Rather than presenting a general requirement platform,
the paper is organized around one research question: how can intelligent agents and
requirement knowledge support reliable detection and analysis of conflicts in
natural language requirements?
\end{abstract}
