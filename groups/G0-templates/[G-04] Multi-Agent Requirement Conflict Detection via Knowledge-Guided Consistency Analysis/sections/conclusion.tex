\section{Conclusion}
\label{conclusion}

This paper studies multi-agent requirement conflict detection as a focused problem
in intelligent requirements engineering. The proposed structure centers on
requirement consistency verification, knowledge-guided conflict analysis, dynamic
conflict detection, and human-in-the-loop refinement. The paper is intended to
evaluate whether a lightweight collaboration among analysis, validation, and review
roles can improve the detection and explanation of semantic, logical, constraint,
role, and temporal conflicts in natural language requirements.
