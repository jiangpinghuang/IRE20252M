\section{Introduction}
\label{introduction}

\subsection{Background and Motivation}
Requirement consistency is a central quality concern in requirements engineering.
Natural language requirements are readable for stakeholders, but they can also encode
semantic conflicts, logical contradictions, incompatible constraints, and conflicting
role expectations. These problems are especially costly when inconsistent
requirements are propagated into design, implementation, and testing.

Recent work has emphasized requirements as a primary interface for AI-assisted
software engineering~\cite{robinsonRequirementsAreAll2025,hanArchCodeIncorporatingSoftware2024}.
At the same time, controlled natural language research shows that reducing ambiguity
while preserving stakeholder readability remains difficult~\cite{darifControlledNaturalLanguage2026}.
This motivates a focused study of requirement consistency verification for natural
language requirements.

\subsection{Challenges of Requirement Conflict Detection}
Requirement conflicts are rarely limited to explicit textual contradictions.
They may appear as implicit incompatibilities between constraints, mismatches between
user roles and permissions, inconsistent temporal ordering, or semantic conflicts
across requirements written in different styles.

This paper focuses on the following research problem:
\begin{quote}
How can multi-agent collaboration and knowledge-guided consistency analysis support
dynamic detection, explanation, and refinement of conflicts in natural language
requirements?
\end{quote}

\subsection{Multi-Agent Requirement Verification}
A single analyzer may miss conflicts that require different reasoning perspectives.
We therefore organize requirement verification as a lightweight collaboration among
analysis, validation, and review roles. The goal is not to build a large agent
platform, but to structure conflict detection as a repeatable workflow that checks
requirement statements against domain knowledge, constraint rules, and stakeholder
feedback.

\subsection{Contributions}
This paper is organized around the following contributions:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item A research problem formulation for multi-agent requirement conflict detection
  via knowledge-guided consistency analysis.
  \item A focused workflow for identifying functional, constraint, role, and temporal
  conflicts in natural language requirements.
  \item A knowledge-guided verification method that combines requirement knowledge,
  constraint rules, semantic patterns, and prompt strategies.
  \item An experimental plan for evaluating conflict detection effectiveness,
  consistency verification accuracy, false positives, and human verification effort.
\end{itemize}
