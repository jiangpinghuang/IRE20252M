\begin{abstract}
Natural-language requirements are often loosely structured, semantically mixed, and difficult to analyze in a uniform representation.
This paper studies the problem of requirement structure modeling: how to transform informal requirement statements into structured, analyzable, and extensible requirement representations.
We outline a knowledge-guided multi-agent workflow that decomposes requirement semantics, constructs a requirement graph, and incorporates human feedback for representation refinement.
The paper is positioned as a research-problem-driven study for intelligent requirements engineering, with evaluation centered on structure completeness, semantic consistency, graph quality, and representation accuracy.
\end{abstract}
