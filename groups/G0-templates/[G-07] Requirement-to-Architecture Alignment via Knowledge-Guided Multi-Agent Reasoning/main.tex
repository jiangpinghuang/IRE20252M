\begin{abstract}
Natural-language requirements often describe expected system behavior without making the architectural implications explicit.
This paper studies the problem of aligning requirements with software architecture through a knowledge-guided multi-agent reasoning workflow.
Rather than proposing a general platform, we focus on how analyst, architecture, and reviewer agents can extract architectural concerns, map requirements to components and constraints, and maintain requirement-to-architecture traceability under human supervision.
The planned evaluation examines whether the workflow improves architecture alignment accuracy, mapping completeness, traceability quality, and architectural consistency compared with non-architecture-aware baselines.
\end{abstract}
