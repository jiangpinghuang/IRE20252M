\section{Related Work}
\label{related_work}

\subsection{Requirement-to-Architecture Mapping}
\label{sec:rw-ra-mapping}

This subsection positions the paper against approaches that connect requirement statements with architectural elements, design responsibilities, interfaces, and quality-attribute decisions.
The discussion should emphasize why architecture-aware mapping is different from general requirement extraction.

\subsection{Architecture Recovery and Software Architecture Reasoning}
\label{sec:rw-architecture-reasoning}

This subsection reviews work on recovering, analyzing, and reasoning about software architecture.
The focus is on architectural views, components, connectors, constraints, and design rationale that can support requirement-to-architecture alignment.

\subsection{Requirement Traceability}
\label{sec:rw-traceability}

This subsection covers trace links between requirements and downstream design artifacts.
The discussion should highlight traceability quality, link completeness, and the role of trace links in validating architectural consistency.

\subsection{LLM-Based Requirement Engineering}
\label{sec:rw-llm-re}

This subsection discusses LLM-based requirement engineering only where it is relevant to architecture awareness.
The focus is on requirement understanding, quality-attribute extraction, and design implication analysis, rather than general LLM capabilities.

\subsection{Multi-Agent Collaboration in Software Engineering}
\label{sec:rw-multi-agent}

This subsection reviews multi-agent collaboration patterns for software engineering tasks.
The discussion should be limited to role separation, cross-checking, and human-supervised reasoning that support requirement-to-architecture mapping.
